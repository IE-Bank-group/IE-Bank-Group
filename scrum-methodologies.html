Scrum Methodology

As the Product Owner for the IE Bank app, the Scrum methodology has been adopted in an approach specifically to ensure high-quality product delivery. I have provided all specific practices and ceremonies that have been implemented during this time.

1. Scrum Framework

The Scrum framework is used to manage the overview development process for the IE Bank app, enabling collaboration and as much transparency with all the team members, to ensure everyone is on the same track.

  • Roles:
    • Product Owner: Responsible for developing the vision/mission statements as well as sprint backlog prioritization and all documentation.
    • Scrum Team: Includes full-stack developers, cloud architects, site reliability engineers, infrastructure engineers, and cybersecurity engineers.
  • Components:
    • Product Backlog: Maintained and developed in Azure DevOps.
    • Sprint Backlog: Defined for each sprint during sprint planning sessions.
  • Events:
    • Sprint Planning
    • Weekly Standup Meetings
    • Sprint Review
    • Sprint Retrospective

2. Ceremonies Conducted

1. Backlog

Description: Backlog overview sessions are held with all team members to refine user stories, ensuring all team members understand what is asked of them.

Outcome: A prioritized and very clear backlog with user stories that have defined acceptance criteria per user story.

Tool Used: Azure DevOps.

2. Sprint Planning

Description: A session where the team decides the sprint goal and selects backlog items for the sprint epics.

Frequency: At the beginning of our sprint.

Outcome: A sprint backlog with well-defined tasks and responsibilities assigned.

Tool Used: Azure DevOps.

3. Weekly Scrum Meetings

Description: A brief meeting held weekly/bi-weekly where the team members share updates regarding their roles and deliverables and their progress. It's important to go through any issues we are facing so we can assist each other and brainstorm ideas.

Outcome: The meetings greatly improved team coordination and fixing issues as efficiently as possible without wasting time.

Tool Used: Slack for updates and Zoom for the meetings (some meetings were done face-to-face).

4. Sprint Review

Description: A session held at the end to demonstrate completed work to stakeholders and gather feedback.

Frequency: End of the sprint.

Outcome: Feedback and presenting to the professors in class.

5. Sprint Retrospective

Description: A reflection session between all team members to identify what went well, what didn’t, and giving recognition to team members who did a great job.

Frequency: End of the sprint.

Tool Used: Azure DevOps Retrospective extension.

3. Tools and Integrations

In order to ensure seamless collaboration between all the team members, we used the following tools:

  • Azure DevOps:
    • Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, and Retrospective.
    • Tracking progress of each epic/feature/user story.
  • GitHub Projects: Managing roadmap and task prioritization/issues.
  • Slack: Team communication and integration with Azure DevOps for notifications.
  • Zoom: Recording all Scrum ceremonies.
  • GitHub Pages: Includes all documentation.

4. Outcomes and Benefits of the Scrum Methodology Followed

  • Work transparency for all team members.
  • Regular feedback loops ensuring continuous improvement and bug fixes.
  • Delivering a high-quality product that aligns perfectly with our MVP and our guidelines.
